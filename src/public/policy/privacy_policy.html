Behavior Tracking


Gateway uses cookies to store information about your Site visit, activities, and preferences. A cookie is a small amount of text that is sent to your browser from a web server and stored on your computer’s hard drive (individual a “Cookie” and collectively “Cookies”). Most modern browsers automatically accept the placement of Cookies on your computer’s hard drive. If you would prefer to prevent your computer from accepting Cookies, you can disable Cookies from your browser; however, this may also prevent you from accessing certain features of the Site.


Like most other commercial websites, the Site may monitor traffic patterns, Site usage and related sites. We may automatically (i.e., not via registration) collect or compile certain information when you access the Site. Examples of this type of information include but are not limited to: the aggregate and unique number of visitors to the Site, the dates and times of visits to the Site, IP addresses and the domains, cities and countries of visitors, the pages of the Site visited and the content accessed, the type and version of the browser and operating system used to access the Site, search engine and search terms used within the Site or to visit the Site, referring sites, and HTTP errors generated.

We may provide such information to Gateway contractors for these purposes. We also may use a web analytics service or services on some pages of the Site to improve web design and usability. This may involve recording mouse clicks, mouse movements, scrolling activity as well as any text you type on the Site. Gateway does not track your browsing habits across websites which do not use the same services.

Such parties collect information about your visits to the Site, and your interaction with the Services. The anonymous information is collected through the use of cookies and pixel tags.
